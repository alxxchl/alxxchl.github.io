Gyeongbokgung Palace

Gyeongbokgung is the largest and arguably the most beautiful of Seoul's Five Grand Palaces, serving as the main royal residence during the Joseon Dynasty. It is worth visiting for its profound historical and cultural significance, allowing you to step back into Korea's regal past. You can go to witness the spectacular changing of the Royal Guard ceremony at the main Gwanghwamun Gate, explore the vast palace grounds, and rent a hanbok (traditional Korean attire) to gain free entry and enhance your experience.

Bukchon Hanok Village

This picturesque neighborhood is located between Gyeongbokgung and Changdeokgung Palaces, featuring hundreds of beautifully preserved traditional Korean houses (hanok). It is worth visiting to experience the charm of a 600-year-old traditional residential area that still functions as a living neighborhood. You can go to walk along its steep, winding streets for iconic photo opportunities, visit small traditional craft workshops, and enjoy scenic views of the surrounding palaces and city skyline.

Myeongdong

Myeongdong is Seoul's bustling central hub for shopping, cosmetics, and street food, attracting millions of tourists every year. It is worth visiting for its incredibly dense concentration of international and Korean beauty brands, fashion retailers, and lively energy. You can go to shop for the latest K-Beauty products, sample a huge variety of Korean street snacks (like tteokbokki and tornado potato) from the evening food stalls, and experience the quintessential Korean shopping culture.

Hongdae

Named after the nearby Hongik University, Hongdae is the heart of Seoul’s youth culture, indie music, and artistic expression. It is worth visiting for its vibrant, bohemian, and energetic atmosphere, particularly appealing to students and young adults. You can go to browse unique street fashion boutiques and vintage shops, catch live performances by buskers and indie bands on the weekend, explore themed cafes, and enjoy the area's active nightlife scene.

Gangnam

Gangnam is synonymous with wealth, high-end fashion, and the peak of modern Korean life, famously highlighted by the global hit "Gangnam Style." It is worth visiting to witness the sharp contrast between the city's historical north and its sleek, modern, affluent south. You can go to shop at luxury department stores like Galleria and COEX Mall, explore the trendy Garosu-gil street for boutique cafes and designer shops, and visit the Starfield COEX Library.

Insadong

Insadong is a charming, traditional district focused on preserving and showcasing Korea's traditional culture, art, and antiques. It is worth visiting for its unique cultural ambiance, where the main street is lined with traditional tea houses, art galleries, and antique shops. You can go to buy traditional crafts and calligraphy, enjoy a quiet cup of traditional Korean tea, explore the multi-story Ssamziegil complex for unique gifts, and feel a deep connection to Korea's artistic heritage.

Dongdaemun Market

Dongdaemun is Seoul's massive, 24-hour shopping district, renowned as the largest fashion and wholesale market in Korea. It is worth visiting for its incredible variety of clothing, accessories, and fabrics at both wholesale and retail prices, especially if you enjoy late-night shopping. You can go to explore the countless modern shopping malls like Doota and Migliore, and experience the lively atmosphere of the traditional night market, making it a paradise for fashionistas and bargain hunters.

N Seoul Tower

Located atop Namsan Mountain, the N Seoul Tower (or Namsan Tower) is one of the city’s most recognizable landmarks and a premier spot for panoramic views. It is worth visiting for the spectacular, unobstructed 360-degree views of the sprawling Seoul metropolitan area, particularly magical at sunset and night. You can go to take the Namsan cable car up the mountain, attach a "love lock" to the surrounding fence areas, and visit the observation deck for a breathtaking perspective on the city.

Gwangjang Market

Gwangjang Market is one of South Korea's oldest and largest traditional markets, primarily famous for its vibrant street food and authentic Korean cuisine. It is worth visiting for a genuine, bustling culinary experience where you can try a huge array of Korean dishes served directly by vendors. You can go to sample local favorites like bindaetteok (mung bean pancakes), mayak kimbap (addictive mini seaweed rolls), and various soups and noodles in a truly traditional and lively setting.
