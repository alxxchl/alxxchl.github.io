Dotonbori

Dotonbori is the dazzling entertainment and gastronomic heart of Osaka, famous for its illuminated signboards, particularly the iconic Glico Running Man. It is worth visiting because it embodies Osaka's nickname, "Kuidaore" (eat until you drop), with countless restaurants and street food stalls offering local specialties like takoyaki (octopus balls) and okonomiyaki (savory pancakes). You can go to take photos of the massive, mechanized crab, fish, and dumpling signs, sample regional cuisine, and enjoy the lively, canal-side atmosphere, especially after sunset.

Osaka Castle

Osaka Castle is one of Japan's most famous landmarks, playing a crucial role in the unification of Japan in the 16th century. It is worth visiting for its deep historical significance and its beautiful multi-tiered architecture, which is especially stunning when surrounded by cherry blossoms in spring. You can go to explore the castle museum inside the main keep (which offers a timeline of the castle's history), walk the vast castle grounds, and admire the formidable stone walls and moats.

Universal Studios Japan

Universal Studios Japan is a major theme park featuring attractions based on popular movies and franchises. It is worth visiting because it is home to world-class, exclusive themed lands, most notably The Wizarding World of Harry Potter and Super Nintendo World, which features interactive experiences based on Mario and his friends. You can go to ride high-energy roller coasters, enjoy spectacular shows, and immerse yourself in intricately detailed movie worlds.

Umeda Sky Building

The Umeda Sky Building is a striking architectural landmark in the Kita (North) district, consisting of two towers connected at the top by a "Floating Garden Observatory." It is worth visiting for its unique, futuristic design and its 360-degree open-air viewing deck. You can go to ascend to the observatory to enjoy romantic sunsets and dazzling night views of the city, and stroll on the "Lumi Sky Walk" where the floor glows in the dark.

Shinsaibashi-suji

Kuromon Market is often dubbed "Osaka's Kitchen," a lively covered public market stretching over 580 meters. It is worth visiting to experience an authentic Japanese food culture hub, where the city's top chefs and home cooks source their ingredients. You can go to sample incredibly fresh seafood (especially oysters, scallops, and tuna), try wagyu beef skewers, browse seasonal fruits, and enjoy casual, delicious meals at the many food stalls and small restaurants.

Shinsekai

Shinsekai (meaning "New World") is an old, colorful neighborhood defined by the retro charm of the Tsutenkaku Tower. It is worth visiting for its distinct, nostalgic atmosphere that recreates a vision of Osaka from the early 20th century. You can go to climb the Tsutenkaku Tower for a view of the area, play games at the local arcades, and try the local specialty, kushikatsu (deep-fried skewers), at one of the many lively restaurants.

Orange Steet

Orange Street, formally known as Tachibana Street, is the major hub for Osaka's street fashion, interior design, and independent youth culture, located in the Namba area. It is worth visiting if you are interested in urban style, Japanese streetwear, unique design boutiques, and vintage shops, offering a cooler, more niche alternative to the mainstream shopping of Shinsaibashi. You can go to explore the trendy multi-brand stores, browse furniture and home goods, and relax at one of the area's many hip cafes.

Namba

Namba serves as the southern gateway and primary transportation, shopping, and entertainment hub of Osaka, often considered the heart of the Minami district. It is worth visiting because it acts as the central point connecting major attractions like Dotonbori, Shinsaibashi, and the Kuromon Market. You can go to shop at large complexes like Takashimaya Department Store and the unique architectural mall Namba Parks, catch a traditional performance at the Namba Grand Kagetsu theater, and easily access all major subway and rail lines.
